Getting My Start in SEO

Spending my days behind a desk optimizing the web wasn't always my plan A, but a series of failures helped me find my true passion for helping people explore the world around them.

By Cooper Hollmaier | May 6, 2020, 11:49am PST

In light of the public health crisis all of us are experiencing, the internships that many journalism and communications students need to graduate have all but dried up. As businesses across the nation struggle to keep their doors open, hiring freezes are running rampant in an effort to curb costs.

Schools around the country (including my own alma mater) have shifted students to a self-study model, in which students are on their own to acquire critical skills they'll need to be successful in the modern workplace. As a result, I've offered to mentor one such student as they aspire to work as an in-house SEO. They remainder of this article is inspired by the brilliant questions I've been asked by someone just dipping their toes in the shallow end of search engine optimization.
